CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the ubiquinol-cytochrome c reductase
CC       complex (complex III or cytochrome b-c1 complex), which is a
CC       respiratory chain that generates an electrochemical potential
CC       coupled to ATP synthesis (By similarity).
CC   -!- COFACTOR: Binds 2 heme groups non-covalently (By similarity).
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The bc1 complex contains 11 subunits: 3 respiratory
CC       subunits (cytochrome b, cytochrome c1 and Rieske/UQCRFS1), 2 core
CC       proteins (UQCRC1/QCR1 and UQCRC2/QCR2) and 6 low-molecular weight
CC       proteins (UQCRH/QCR6, UQCRB/QCR7, UQCRQ/QCR8, UQCR10/QCR9,
CC       UQCR11/QCR10 and a cleavage product of Rieske/UQCRFS1).
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion inner membrane; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein (By similarity).
CC   -!- DISEASE: Note=Defects in MT-CYB are a rare cause of mitochondrial
CC       dysfunction underlying different myopathies. They include
CC       mitochondrial encephalomyopathy, hypertrophic cardiomyopathy
CC       (HCM), and sporadic mitochondrial myopathy (MM). In mitochondrial
CC       myopathy, exercise intolerance is the predominant symptom.
CC       Additional features include lactic acidosis, muscle weakness
CC       and/or myoglobinuria. Defects in MTCYB are also found in cases of
CC       exercise intolerance accompanied by deafness, mental retardation,
CC       retinitis pigmentosa, cataract, growth retardation, epilepsy
CC       (multisystem disorder).
CC   -!- DISEASE: Defects in MT-CYB are the cause of cardiomyopathy
CC       infantile histiocytoid (CMIH) [MIM:500000]. CMIH is characterized
CC       by the presence of pale granular foamy histiocyte-like cells
CC       within the myocardium. It usually affects children younger than 2
CC       years of age, with a clear predominance of females over males.
CC       Infants present with dysrhythmia or cardiac arrest, and the
CC       clinical course is usually fulminant, sometimes simulating sudden
CC       infant death syndrome.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Defects in MT-CYB contribute to Leber hereditary optic
CC       neuropathy (LHON) [MIM:535000]. LHON is a maternally inherited
CC       disease resulting in acute or subacute loss of central vision, due
CC       to optic nerve dysfunction. Cardiac conduction defects and
CC       neurological defects have also been described in some patients.
CC       LHON results from primary mitochondrial DNA mutations affecting
CC       the respiratory chain complexes.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Heme 1 (or BL or b562) is low-potential and absorbs
CC       at about 562 nm, and heme 2 (or BH or b566) is high-potential and
CC       absorbs at about 566 nm (By similarity).
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the cytochrome b family.
