CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mediates pre-mRNA alternative splicing regulation. Acts
CC       either as activator or repressor of splicing on specific pre-mRNA
CC       targets. Inhibits cardiac troponin-T (TNNT2) pre-mRNA exon
CC       inclusion but induces insulin receptor (IR) pre-mRNA exon
CC       inclusion in muscle. Antagonizes the alternative splicing activity
CC       pattern of CELF proteins. Regulates the TNNT2 exon 5 skipping
CC       through competition with U2AF2. Inhibits the formation of the
CC       spliceosome A complex on intron 4 of TNNT2 pre-mRNA. Binds to the
CC       stem-loop structure within the polypyrimidine tract of TNNT2
CC       intron 4 during spliceosome assembly. Binds to the 5'-YGCU(U/G)Y-
CC       3'consensus sequence. Binds to the IR RNA. Binds to expanded CUG
CC       repeat RNA, which folds into a hairpin structure containing GC
CC       base pairs and bulged, unpaired U residues.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with DDX1 and YBX1. Interacts with HNRNPH1; the
CC       interaction in RNA-independent.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus. Cytoplasm. Cytoplasmic granule.
CC       Note=Localized with DDX1, TIAL1 and YBX1 in stress granules upon
CC       stress. Localized in the cytoplasm of multinucleated myotubes.
CC       Colocalizes with nuclear foci of retained expanded-repeat
CC       transcripts in myotubes from patients affected by myotonic
CC       dystrophy.

CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highly expressed in cardiac, skeletal muscle

CC   -!- DISEASE: Plays a role in the pathogenesis of dystrophia myotonica
CC       type 1 (DM1) [MIM:160900]. A muscular disorder characterized by
CC       myotonia, muscle wasting in the distal extremities, cataract,
CC       hypogonadism, defective endocrine functions, male baldness and
CC       cardiac arrhythmias. Note=In muscle cells from DM1 patients, MBNL1
CC       is sequestered by DMPK RNAs containing CUG triplet repeat
CC       expansions. MBNL1 binding is proportional to repeat length
CC       consistent with the direct correlation between the length of
CC       repeat expansion and disease severity.
