CC   -!- FUNCTION: Hydrolyzes cerebroside sulfate.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: A cerebroside 3-sulfate + H(2)O = a
CC       cerebroside + sulfate.
CC   -!- COFACTOR: Binds 1 calcium ion per subunit.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Inhibited by phosphate. The phosphate forms a
CC       covalent bond with the active site 3-oxoalanine.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer at neutral pH and homooctamer at acidic pH.
CC       Exists both as a single chain of 58 kDa (component A) or as a
CC       chain of 50 kDa (component B) linked by disulfide bond(s) to a 7
CC       kDa chain (component C). Interacts with SUMF1.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Lysosome.
CC   -!- PTM: The conversion to 3-oxoalanine (also known as C-
CC       formylglycine, FGly), of a serine or cysteine residue in
CC       prokaryotes and of a cysteine residue in eukaryotes, is critical
CC       for catalytic activity. This post-translational modification is
CC       severely defective in multiple sulfatase deficiency (MSD).
CC   -!- DISEASE: Defects in ARSA are a cause of leukodystrophy
CC       metachromatic (MLD) [MIM:250100]. MLD is a disease due to a
CC       lysosomal storage defect. It is characterized by intralysosomal
CC       storage of cerebroside-3-sulfate in neural and non-neural tissues,
CC       with a diffuse loss of myelin in the central nervous system.
CC       Progressive demyelination causes a variety of neurological
CC       symptoms, including gait disturbances, ataxias, optical atrophy,
CC       dementia, seizures, and spastic tetraparesis. Three forms of the
CC       disease can be distinguished according to the age at onset: late-
CC       infantile, juvenile and adult.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Arylsulfatase A activity is defective in multiple
CC       sulfatase deficiency (MSD) [MIM:272200]. A clinically and
CC       biochemically heterogeneous disorder caused by the simultaneous
CC       impairment of all sulfatases, due to defective post-translational
CC       modification and activation. It combines features of individual
CC       sulfatase deficiencies such as metachromatic leukodystrophy,
CC       mucopolysaccharidosis, chondrodysplasia punctata, hydrocephalus,
CC       ichthyosis, neurologic deterioration and developmental delay.
CC       Note=Arylsulfatase A activity is impaired in multiple sulfatase
CC       deficiency due to mutations in SUMF1. SUMF1 mutations result in
CC       defective post-translational modification of ARSA at residue Cys-
CC       69 that is not converted to 3-oxoalanine.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The metal cofactor was first identified as
CC       magnesium ion, based on the structure of the recombinant protein,
CC       but when purified from human placenta, the protein contains 1
CC       calcium ion per subunit.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the sulfatase family.
