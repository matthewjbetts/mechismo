CC   -!- FUNCTION: Has a dehydrogenase activity on palmitoyl-CoA (C16:0)
CC       and stearoyl-CoA (C18:0). It is three times more active on
CC       palmitoyl-CoA than on stearoyl-CoA. Has little activity on
CC       octanoyl-CoA (C8:0), butyryl-CoA (C4:0) or isovaleryl-CoA (5:0).
CC   -!- COFACTOR: FAD (By similarity).
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion (Potential).
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Ubiquitously expressed in most normal human
CC       tissues and cancer cell lines with high level of expression in
CC       heart, skeletal muscles, brain, kidney and liver.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Defects in ACAD9 are a cause of acyl-CoA dehydrogenase
CC       family member type 9 deficiency (ACAD9 deficiency) [MIM:611126].
CC       ACAD9 deficiency patients present with episodic liver dysfunction
CC       during otherwise mild illnesses or cardiomyopathy, along with
CC       chronic neurologic dysfunction.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the acyl-CoA dehydrogenase family.
