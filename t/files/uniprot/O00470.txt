CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as a transcriptional regulator of PAX6. Acts as a
CC       transcriptional activator of PF4 in complex with PBX1 or PBX2.
CC       Required for hematopoiesis, megakaryocyte lineage development and
CC       vascular patterning. May function as a cofactor for HOXA7 and
CC       HOXA9 in the induction of myeloid leukemias.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with the N-terminal region of PBX1 to form a
CC       heterodimer which binds DNA including a cAMP-responsive sequence
CC       in CYP17. Also forms heterodimers with PBX2. Forms heterotrimers
CC       with PBX1 or PBX2 and a number of HOX proteins including HOXA9,
CC       HOXD4 and HOXD9 where it acts as a non-DNA-binding partner. Also
CC       forms heterotrimers with PBX1 and HOX proteins including HOXD9 and
CC       HOXD10 where PBX1 is the non-DNA-binding partner.

CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed at low level in normal
CC       immunohepatopoietic tissues, including the fetal liver. Expressed

CC       expression seen in those with a megakaryocytic-erythroid
CC       phenotype. Also expressed at high levels in the cerebellum.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Defects in MEIS1 could be a cause of susceptibility to
CC       restless legs syndrome type 7 (RLS7) [MIM:612853]. Restless legs
CC       syndrome (RLS) is a neurologic sleep/wake disorder characterized
CC       by uncomfortable and unpleasant sensations in the legs that appear
CC       at rest, usually at night, inducing an irresistible desire to move
CC       the legs. The disorder results in nocturnal insomnia and chronic
CC       sleep deprivation.
