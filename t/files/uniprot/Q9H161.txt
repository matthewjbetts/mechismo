CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transcription factor involved in skull and limb
CC       development. Plays an essential role in craniofacial development,
CC       skin and hair follicle development.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Binds DNA (By similarity).
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expression is likely to be restricted to bone.
CC       Found in parietal bone.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Defects in ALX4 are the cause of parietal foramina 2
CC       (PFM2) [MIM:609597]; also known as foramina parietalia permagna
CC       (FPP). PFM2 is an autosomal dominant disease characterized by oval
CC       defects of the parietal bones caused by deficient ossification
CC       around the parietal notch, which is normally obliterated during
CC       the fifth fetal month. PFM2 is also a clinical feature of Potocki-
CC       Shaffer syndrome.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Defects in ALX4 are the cause of frontonasal dysplasia
CC       type 2 (FND2) [MIM:613451]. The term frontonasal dysplasia
CC       describes an array of abnormalities affecting the eyes, forehead
CC       and nose and linked to midfacial dysraphia. The clinical picture
CC       is highly variable. Major findings include true ocular
CC       hypertelorism; broadening of the nasal root; median facial cleft
CC       affecting the nose and/or upper lip and palate; unilateral or
CC       bilateral clefting of the alae nasi; lack of formation of the
CC       nasal tip; anterior cranium bifidum occultum; a V-shaped or
CC       widow's peak frontal hairline.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Involved in Potocki-Shaffer syndrome (PSS) [MIM:601224].
CC       PSS is a contiguous gene syndrome caused by deletion of the
CC       11p11.2 region.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the paired homeobox family.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Contains 1 homeobox DNA-binding domain.
